About Me

My name is Carolina del Mar Gamez Herazo, so yes, “Del Mar” is my middle name and despite what you may think, I really feel lucky for it, it is original and cute. I was born in Cartagena, my beautiful “ciudad amurallada”, land of beaches, champeta, history and a lot of culture.
I studied my whole life at Colegio La Anunciación, where I learned the basics of my knowledge and I finished a technician at SENA in administrative assistance just before graduating in 2020. I started medicine at Universidad del Norte, but dropped out because although I really like science, part of me also liked numbers and math (yes, I'm crazy like that). A semester later, I started biotechnological engineering at EIA University, where I am now in my seventh semester.
These last semesters I had the opportunity to find my favorite area of the career, so at the moment I am fully involved in the world of bioprocesses, including modeling, simulation and control. So, with a solid academic background, I hope to push the boundaries of scientific innovation through proper use of existing techniques and methodologies.
My background includes participation in iGEM Design League, where I collaborated in the development of a solution for the control of Thumbergia alata using synthetic biology. I have actively participated in multiple research groups, such as bioprocess modeling, bioinformatics, genetic engineering and fermentation and applied bioprocesses. I have also taken courses in specialized areas of biotechnology and engineering such as design of experiments, bioprocess modeling and industrial process simulation.
With each experience, I have strengthened my passion for biotechnology and its potential to drive meaningful change. Looking ahead, I am eager to continue expanding my expertise and my knowledge, and becoming a great person and professional.

Projects

Winogradsky column

Winogradsky column simulates the microbes ecosystems found in fresh and seawater. This column differentiates in three characteristic habitats: aerobic zone in the surface, microaerophilic zone in the middle, and anaerobic zone in the bottom. So a winogradsky column was built in a plastic bottle and the results were studied.

Biofertilizer

Nitrogen is essential for plants. Rhizobium bacteria can fixate this nutrient into the roots of legumes. Taking this into account, rhizobium bacteria were isolated to formulate a biofertilizer, to improve the growth of bean plants.

Control of invasive plant

Black-eyed Susan vine, a climbing plant from east Africa, has propagated through all America becoming an aggressive invasive species. A gene silencing strategy based on small interfering RNA (siRNA) is proposed for the control of this invasive species.
